# Generative/World-Model-Based Composition & Interpolation of Eval Scenarios — Literature Report (through mid-2026)

> Research compiled 2026-07-03 for the scenario-set-construction feature (follow-up question:
> can the world model's continuous-interpolation capability compose/blend scenarios into more
> representative or more informative evals?). Companion to
> `scenario-set-construction-sota.md`.

## Executive summary

The literature has converged on **composition as the dominant operator for synthetic eval generation** (skill-crossing, subtask chaining, graph perturbation, tool-sequence evolution), with validation converging on a standard triad: **programmatic/decomposed solvability checks, difficulty targeting into a "learnable band" (ZPD / pass-rate window), and judge+human agreement audits**. Three findings matter most for this harness:

1. **Composition is a measurably distinct axis.** Multiple 2024–2026 results (SKILL-MIX, CompWoB, AgentCoMa, TASTE) show agents lose 25–50 points when known-passable atomic tasks are composed — i.e., composed evals surface real capability gaps that single-intent evals saturate on. TASTE (May 2026) showed frontier agents dropping from 0.82–0.94 to 0.28–0.61 on composed tool-sequence tasks in the *same* domains they had "solved."
2. **True latent-space interpolation of eval items is nearly unexplored as an *evaluation* technique.** Embedding interpolation + LLM decoding exists (MDIT, LM-mixup, inversedMixup) but only for *training* data, validated by downstream gains — nobody has published calibrated, validity-checked *interpolated eval items*. The closest validated analogs are parametric/graph perturbation families (DyVal, DARG, GSM-Symbolic, BeTaL) and IRT-based difficulty calibration of generated items. This is a genuine open niche.
3. **World models are now used for evaluation, not just training** — ADWM (off-policy eval of LLM agents in a diffusion world model), Simmer (latent-failure benchmarking against a symbolic world model), CounterScene (counterfactual safety-critical eval in a generative driving world model) — but **none yet uses the world model's interpolation capability to synthesize novel *composed* eval scenarios**. The universally acknowledged threat: eval conclusions inherit world-model drift and leniency; mitigations are per-step generation (ADWM), minimal-intervention counterfactuals (CounterScene), and grounding in curated/real dynamics (Simmer).

## 1. Task/scenario composition for LLM & agent evals

- **SKILL-MIX** ([arXiv 2310.17567](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.17567), Oct 2023, ICLR 2024). Random k-subsets of N=101 skills × 100 topics; N^k combinatorics guarantees items unlike training data. Operator: *skill-crossing*. Validation: rubric-based GPT-4 grading with deduction points + human spot-check agreement; performance decays sharply in k, and the paper argues k≥3 success evidences generalization beyond "stochastic parrot" behavior.
- **Evol-Instruct / WizardLM** ([arXiv 2304.12244](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.12244), Apr 2023) and **Auto Evol-Instruct** ([arXiv 2406.00770](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.00770), Jun 2024). Operators: *in-depth evolution* (add constraints, deepen, concretize, increase reasoning steps) + *in-breadth mutation*; elimination filter discards failed evolutions. Auto Evol-Instruct meta-optimizes the evolving operators themselves via trajectory analysis. Follow-up: smaller models are better evolvers ([arXiv 2412.11231](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.11231)).
- **DyVal** ([ICLR 2024](https://openreview.net/forum?id=gjfOL9z5Xr)) composes reasoning problems from DAG primitives with complexity constraints; **DyVal2/Meta-Probing-Agents** and **DARG** perturb the extracted reasoning graph of existing items; **GSM-Symbolic** instantiates templates with variable fills. Operator: *graph/parameter perturbation*; validation is by construction (programmatic ground truth). Survey of the static→dynamic shift: [arXiv 2502.17521](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.17521v2).
- **Benchmark Self-Evolving** ([arXiv 2402.11443](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.11443), Feb 2024). Six reframing operations (context noising, question paraphrase, sub-ability probing…) executed by a multi-agent system with a *judgment agent verifying each evolved instance*; observed general score declines vs. original benchmarks.
- **AutoBencher** ([arXiv 2407.08351](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.08351), Jul 2024). Declarative desiderata (salience, difficulty, novelty) cast as optimization; LLM proposes/refines dataset descriptions. Produced +22% model errors and 27% rank-correlation reduction vs. human benchmarks. Validation: privileged-information generation (generator gets docs the test-taker doesn't) for correctness.
- **CHASE** ([arXiv 2502.14678](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14678), Feb 2025). Bottom-up composition of hard problems from *independently verifiable sub-components* — the cleanest published statement of the "compose then verify each part" pattern; SOTA models land at 40–60%.
- **τ²-bench compositional task generator** ([github](https://github.com/sierra-research/tau2-bench), Jun 2025). Atomic telecom subtasks programmatically aggregated into compound troubleshooting episodes with programmatic success criteria — direct precedent for multi-goal episode composition with built-in verifiability.
- **OdysseyBench / HomerAgents** ([arXiv 2508.09124](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09124), Aug 2025). Multi-agent pipeline (environment exploration → task generation → dialogue synthesis) composing atomic office-app tasks into 602 long-horizon workflows; explicitly motivated by atomic benchmarks overestimating capability.
- **TASTE** ([arXiv 2605.28556](https://hf.co/papers/2605.28556), May 2026). Inverts construction: start from *tool sequences*, sample diverse valid combinations via an adaptive contrastive n-gram model trained on LLM-judged validity signals; then clustering (representativeness) + iterative difficulty evolution. Built τ^c-Bench on τ²-bench domains; >2× unique tool-combination coverage; frontier scores collapse (0.82–0.94 → 0.28–0.61), showing prior scores reflected compositional under-coverage.
- **ProEvolve** ([arXiv 2603.05910](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.05910), Mar 2026). Represents the environment as a *typed relational graph* (data + tools + schema); evolution operators are graph transformations (add/remove/modify capability) that coherently propagate; tasks constructed by *subgraph sampling and instantiation*, validated for executability. The closest published analog to "state-space composition" for agent environments.
- **EvoTD** ([arXiv 2605.11666](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.11666), May 2026). Directed search over a dual-axis manifold (algorithmic skills × complexity attributes) with a *crossover operator* (skill combination) and *parametric mutation* (scale input size/depth); a **Zone-of-Proximal-Development filter** keeps generated tasks in the learnable band — the current best-practice difficulty gate.
- Also: **BeTaL** ([arXiv 2510.25039](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.25039v1), Oct 2025) — LLM-in-the-loop tuning of benchmark template parameters to hit target difficulty within 5.3–13.2% deviation (2–4× better than baselines), applied to τ-bench; **Fine-Grained Benchmark Generation** ([arXiv 2605.18824](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.18824), May 2026) — solution-graph-driven generation with expert-audited error rates lower than MMLU/GSM8K; **InfoSynth** ([arXiv 2601.00575](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00575), Jan 2026) — genetic-algorithm synthesis guided by KL/entropy novelty metrics, self-verifying (97% correct tests/solutions); **MACEval** ([arXiv 2511.09139](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.09139)) and **KBE-DME** ([arXiv 2510.21182](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.21182)) for continual/evolving evaluation.

**Composition operators in use:** skill-crossing (SKILL-MIX, EvoTD), subtask chaining into episodes (τ², OdysseyBench, CHASE), constraint-merging/deepening (Evol-Instruct), structural graph perturbation (DyVal/DARG/ProEvolve), tool-sequence evolution (TASTE), template-parameter scaling (GSM-Symbolic, BeTaL). **Validation patterns:** decomposition into independently verifiable parts; programmatic executability; LLM-judge validity signals distilled into a cheap filter model (TASTE); ZPD/difficulty-band filters; judge+human agreement audits; expert error-rate audits.

## 2. Unsupervised environment design / auto-curricula

- **Classic line:** POET ([arXiv 1901.01753](https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.01753)) — co-evolution of environments and agents with minimal-criterion filtering (solvable-by-someone, not-too-easy); **PAIRED** ([arXiv 2012.02096](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.02096)) — adversary maximizes *regret* (antagonist return − protagonist return), which provably steers generation to solvable-but-unsolved environments; **ACCEL** ([arXiv 2203.01302](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.01302)) — evolve/mutate previously curated levels, curate by regret estimate. Regret is the field's canonical "informative scenario" objective; a learned-adversary stabilization follow-up is [arXiv 2308.10797](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2308.10797).
- **LLM-era UED:** **OMNI-EPIC** ([arXiv 2405.15568](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.15568), ICLR 2025) — LLM generates tasks *as code* + a "model of human interestingness" filter with RAG against a task archive (learnability + novelty replacing regret); **EnvGen** ([arXiv 2403.12014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.12014)) — LLM adapts environment configs from agent failure feedback; **Eurekaverse** ([PMLR v270](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v270/liang25a.html) / [arXiv 2411.01775](https://arxiv.org/html/2411.01775v1), CoRL 2025) — LLM samples progressively harder, learnable environment code (parkour), beats human-designed curricula.
- **2025–2026, applied to LLM agents:** **DreamGym** ([arXiv 2511.03773](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03773), Nov 2025 — this harness's ancestor) does UED-style curriculum *inside the world model*: adaptive task generation targeting the current policy's frontier plus an evolving replay buffer. **COvolve** ([arXiv 2603.28386](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.28386), Mar 2026) — two-player zero-sum co-evolution of LLM-generated Python environments and policies with mixed-strategy Nash meta-policies (regret-flavored, code-level). **BeTaL** explicitly imports "environment design principles" into benchmark design. **Agent World Model** ([arXiv 2602.10090](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.10090v1), Feb 2026) scales fully synthetic executable tool-use environments for agentic RL. The **Agentic Environment Engineering survey** ([arXiv 2606.12191](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.12191), Jun 2026) taxonomizes environment synthesis (symbolic vs. neural) and names *difficulty-driven environment evolution* as one of three evolution paradigms.
- **Takeaway:** in the LLM era, exact regret is replaced by cheaper proxies — pass-rate bands (accept tasks with panel success ∈ (0,1)), success-variance/entropy, "solvable-by-strong-model, failed-by-target" gaps, and interestingness filters. No published work yet uses regret-style selection over *world-model-imagined* eval scenarios for LLM agents — DreamGym does it for training; porting it to eval-set construction is open.

## 3. World models as scenario generators

**Autonomous driving (most mature validity machinery):**
- **DiffScene** ([AAAI 2025](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/32951)) — diffusion prior for realism + adversarial optimization objectives under budgets, explicitly trading off safety-criticality against staying on the data manifold.
- **From Words to Collisions** ([arXiv 2502.02145](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.02145), Feb 2025) — LLM-guided generation *and evaluation* of safety-critical scenarios; **LD-Scene** (2025) — LLM-guided diffusion for controllable adversarial scenarios; **steerable adversarial generation via test-time preference alignment** ([arXiv 2509.20102](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.20102)); survey: [arXiv 2506.11526](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.11526).
- **CounterScene** ([arXiv 2603.21104](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.21104), Mar 2026) — the key reference. A closed-loop generative BEV world model generates *counterfactual* safety-critical scenarios: identify the causally critical agent, then apply **minimal interventions** (strip its spatial/temporal safety margins) and let risk *emerge through natural interaction propagation* in the world model rather than by unconstrained perturbation. Validity = realism metrics (ADE 1.88 vs 2.09) + zero-shot transfer to nuPlan, while nearly doubling collision rate (12.3%→22.7%). Resolves the adversarialness-vs-realism trade-off via causal minimality — directly portable to LLM world models.
- **Off-policy / world-model evaluation of LLM agents:** **ADWM** ([arXiv 2606.05558](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.05558), Jun 2026) — evaluates a *new* LLM agent policy purely from pre-collected trajectories: latent diffusion world model where each transition is an independent denoising step (limits compounding error) and the evaluated policy conditions the score function; reports reliable value estimates across multi-turn agent tasks. First clean "world model as eval harness" result for LLM agents. **Simmer** ([arXiv 2606.14574](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.14574), Jun 2026) — benchmarks *latent* failures (silent goal degradation, irreversible consequences) by executing LLM plans against a human-curated symbolic world model (77 actions/262 objects/~46.8k interactions); frontier models: only 17% error-free, up to 56% latent failures. Handles world-model validity by *curation*, not learning.
- **LLM world models as environments:** DreamGym ([arXiv 2511.03773](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03773)); **Qwen-AgentWorld** ([arXiv 2606.24597](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.24597)) — trained language world model usable as a decoupled environment simulator across 7 domains; **LLMs as Scalable General-Purpose Simulators** ([arXiv 2510.14969](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.14969v1)); **Self-Evolving World Models for planning** ([arXiv 2606.30639](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.30639v1)); the **Text World Models survey** ([arXiv 2606.09032](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.09032), Jun 2026) explicitly notes that "the world model has become an evaluation protocol" and warns that *benchmark conclusions are only as reliable as the simulator*: rollout drift misleads agents, and verifiers judging from wrongly-predicted states reject correct actions.
- **Gap:** no published work yet uses a learned world model to synthesize *novel composed eval scenarios* for LLM agents (as opposed to training rollouts, off-policy eval on fixed tasks, or driving counterfactuals). The proposal sits in unclaimed territory at the intersection of CounterScene (counterfactual minimal-intervention eval) and TASTE/ProEvolve (compositional task synthesis).

## 4. Interpolation in task/latent space

- **MDIT** ([arXiv 2504.07288](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.07288), Apr 2025) — model-free *task interpolation* in embedding space blends task distributions to create new instruction-tuning tasks, with diversity-based clustering; validated only by downstream training gains.
- **LM-mixup** ([arXiv 2510.20449](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.20449), Oct 2025) and **inversedMixup** ([arXiv 2601.21543](https://arxiv.org/html/2601.21543), Jan 2026) — bridge embedding-level mixup and token-level generation: mix sentence embeddings, then treat the mixed embedding as a soft token and *invert it through the LLM* to decode a natural-language example, with soft labels from interpolation weights. The "interpolate-then-decode" recipe for generating items *between* two existing items.
- **Instruction embedding (PIE)** ([arXiv 2409.19680](https://arxiv.org/html/2409.19680)) — task-identification-oriented instruction embeddings; the natural substrate for interpolation along *task intent* rather than surface form. **Task arithmetic** ([tangent-space analysis, NeurIPS 2023](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/file/d28077e5ff52034cd35b4aa15320caea-Paper-Conference.pdf)) is weight-space, not item-space, but establishes that task combinations compose approximately linearly.
- **Calibration evidence is the weak link.** No paper demonstrates latent-interpolated eval *items* are valid/calibrated. Nearest evidence: (a) **isomorphic physics problem generation** ([arXiv 2602.05114](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.05114)) validates generated variants psychometrically; (b) **IRT-based difficulty estimation via LLM student simulation** ([arXiv 2601.09953](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.09953)) and **LLM respondents for item calibration** ([BJET 2025](https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/bjet.13570); ensembles needed) show generated items *can* be difficulty-calibrated with model panels; (c) **IrtNet** ([arXiv 2510.00844](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.00844)) gives compact IRT ability representations. Interpolation machinery exists; item-validity methodology must be imported from psychometrics.

## 5. Compositional generalization measurement

- **Compositionality gap** (Press et al., [arXiv 2210.03350](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03350)) — models answer sub-questions but fail their composition ~40% of the time.
- **CompWoB** ([arXiv 2311.18751](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.18751)) — web agents drop from 85.4% to 54.8% success on *composed* sequential tasks built from individually-passed atomic tasks; order reversal degrades further.
- **AgentCoMa** ([arXiv 2508.19988](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2508.19988), Aug 2025) — *mixed-type* composition (commonsense + math): ~80% accuracy on individual steps, 51% on composites — a 29-point gap **just as large for reasoning-trained and RL-tuned models**. Failure is specifically at the *type boundary*: heterogeneous constraint-merging is the hardest operator.
- **SKILL-MIX** decay in k, **TASTE**'s coverage result, and **OdysseyBench**'s atomic-vs-workflow deltas triangulate: single-intent evals systematically overestimate deployed capability.
- **Representativeness link:** direct evidence that composed items predict production performance is thin. The 2026 agentic-evaluation review ([Springer AI Review](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-026-11571-0)) reports only ~41% transfer of WebArena success to real websites; surveys flag "predicts real-world performance" as an open need. Composed evals are currently justified as measuring a *distinct axis* (compositional robustness), not as proxies for production traffic.
- **Failure-mode composition** is nascent: **When Tools Fail** ([arXiv 2606.05806](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.05806), Jun 2026) benchmarks dynamic replanning under injected tool anomalies; **Instruction Bleed** ([arXiv 2606.26356](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.26356)) measures interference when multiple prompt modules co-reside; MAS-FIRE (2026) studies compositional fault propagation in multi-agent systems. Nobody has published "combine two known failure triggers from telemetry and test the intersection."

## 6. Validity threats & mitigations

| Threat | Evidence | Mitigations in the literature |
|---|---|---|
| **Unsolvable composites** (merged constraints conflict) | CHASE motivation; ProEvolve executability dimension | Decompose into independently verifiable sub-tasks (CHASE); programmatic executability filters (τ², ProEvolve); self-verification (InfoSynth 97%); solvability rollouts (DreamGym-style) |
| **Difficulty explosion** (composites collapse to 0% — uninformative) | AgentCoMa 29-pt gap; CompWoB | ZPD filters keeping tasks in the learnable band (EvoTD); target-difficulty tuning (BeTaL, 5–13% deviation); iterative difficulty evolution with model panels (TASTE); IRT calibration via simulated-student panels |
| **Distribution drift from production traffic** | Synthetic-data quality survey ([2601.17717](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.17717)) | Seed generation from real traces; salience desiderata (AutoBencher); cluster to representative combinations before evolving (TASTE); realism anchoring (CounterScene's ADE + nuPlan transfer) |
| **Judge unreliability on composites** | Finding Blind Spots in Evaluator LLMs ([2406.13439](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.13439)) | Checklist decomposition per subgoal; rubric + human agreement audit (SKILL-MIX); verification agents per evolved instance; programmatic verifiers wherever possible |
| **Eval overfitting / self-preference** | 2026 practitioner literature | Different model family for generation vs. evaluation; multi-model panels |
| **Single-metric compression hiding reliability** | Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability ([2602.16666](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.16666)) | Multi-run consistency, perturbation robustness alongside success rate (consonant with the k=3 rule) |
| **World-model rollout drift & leniency** | Text World Models survey ([2606.09032](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.09032)) | Per-transition generation (ADWM); curated symbolic dynamics (Simmer); minimal-intervention counterfactuals (CounterScene); spot-check against real environment |

## Application to this harness

Current pipeline (cluster → select → synthesize → verify via solvability rollouts + checklist judges) *selects and re-renders* observed scenarios. Three concrete composition/interpolation mechanisms, in increasing order of novelty risk:

### Mechanism A — Cross-cluster task merging with WM-verified joint solvability
Pick scenario pairs (A, B) from *different* telemetry clusters. World model synthesizes a **unioned seed state** (ProEvolve-style typed union of entities/tools/records, with explicit constraint-conflict resolution before rendering). Compose intents under a declared **dependency type** — independent, sequential (B consumes A's output), or resource-contended (the most informative per AgentCoMa).
*Verification extensions:* constraint-conflict audit before rollouts (LLM leniency renders impossible unions happily); EvoTD-style ZPD band on the k=3 panel pass rate (accept ∈ [0.1, 0.9]); per-subgoal checklists with a joint-consistency item ("did solving B corrupt A's outcome?"); bidirectional back-agreement (decompose composite → must match both sources and nothing else).

### Mechanism B — Intent-interpolation families (boundary mapping)
For same/adjacent-cluster pairs, interpolate intent embeddings at α ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 0.75} and decode (inversedMixup recipe, or textual blend prompting). The WM renders a plausible seed state at each intermediate intent even though no trace exists there. Product = a **monotone family** from A to B — valuable as a **boundary map** (find the α where agent behavior flips), not as "representative" items.
*Verification extensions:* typicality gate against the telemetry corpus (reject interpolants beyond the 95th-percentile embedding distance of real traces); difficulty-monotonicity across the family (a spike at α=0.5 = incoherent hybrid → reject); round-trip back-agreement (re-embed decoded intent, must lie between endpoints at ~α); IRT panel calibration to share a scale with telemetry-derived items.

### Mechanism C — Failure-trigger composition via minimal counterfactual intervention (CounterScene-for-agents)
Mine telemetry for known failure triggers (tool timeout, stale state, ambiguous entity, mid-task policy change). Take a scenario the agent passes; WM applies a **minimal intervention** injecting one trigger, then compose *two* triggers whose interaction is causally plausible. CounterScene discipline: intervene minimally, let the failure opportunity *emerge through the WM's natural dynamics*. Fills the open "failure-mode composition" niche.
*Verification extensions:* fault-persistence checker (LLM WMs forget injected faults mid-rollout); PAIRED-style admission (reference-strong-agent rollout must succeed with documented recovery); paired unperturbed control rollout so every composite reads out as a *delta*; recovery-behavior checklist items; strict generator/judge/WM family separation.

**Cross-cutting:** before trusting any composed set for regression tracking, hold out recent traffic and check composite-set scores **rank agents consistently with telemetry-derived scores while adding discrimination** (TASTE's analysis is the template). If composites re-rank agents wildly, report them as a separate compositional-robustness axis rather than folding into the representative set — the literature says that's what they actually measure.
